# NGOs: Community & Pro-Bono Legal Services

Community legal centers and pro-bono legal services provide critical access to justice for vulnerable individuals. Operating on limited resources, these services must rapidly verify a client's eligibility, gather complex legal histories, and manage highly privileged information securely. 

A Solid Databox allows clients to maintain an irrefutable, portable ledger of their legal standing and evidence without burdening underfunded NGOs with massive data storage costs or security liabilities.

## Core Legal Data Payloads

### 1. Evidence & Provenance Gathering
- **Immutable Evidence Ledgers:** Clients can deposit time-stamped, cryptographically signed digital evidence (e.g., photos of property damage, threatening text messages, or eviction notices) directly into their Databox. Because the provenance of these Verifiable Credentials (VCs) is mathematically intact, community lawyers can instantly trust the timeline of events.
- **Police & Court Records:** Seamless integration with government justice portals allows clients to securely pull official police reports, apprehended violence orders (AVOs), or court transcripts into their Pod. This maintains a single, unalterable source of truth for their legal counsel.

### 2. Client Eligibility & Means Testing
- **Instant Means Testing:** Pro-bono services are strictly means-tested based on income and assets. Instead of gathering months of physical bank statements (which expose irrelevant personal spending), clients grant temporary read-access to income and welfare VCs (e.g., government subsidy proofs). This allows the legal center to instantly verify that the client qualifies for free assistance without permanently retaining their sensitive financial data.

### 3. Legal Privilege & ZKP Conflict Checking
- **Privileged Communications:** All communication between the client and the pro-bono lawyer occurs via the Databox's secure, encrypted messaging layer, ensuring absolute legal privilege and preventing interception by hostile parties.
- **Zero-Knowledge Proof (ZKP) Conflict Checks:** Legal services are bound by strict ethical rules to ensure they do not represent opposing parties in a dispute. ZKP protocols can query the Databox to mathematically guarantee no conflicts of interest exist (e.g., ensuring the center hasn't previously advised the client's abuser) without ever disclosing the opposing party's actual identity or compromising confidentiality.
