# Commercial: Clothing and Footwear Applications

This vertical focuses on the retail ecosystem for apparel, footwear, and related accessories. Integrating a Solid Databox in this sector allows consumers to maintain a secure, private, and portable profile that deeply enhances their shopping experience while respecting their data sovereignty.

## Core Consumer Data Payloads

In a Solid-enabled retail environment, the databox stores several critical types of personal, medical, and transactional data:

### 1. Personal Physiology & Health Requirements
Consumers can maintain verified physiological data that retailers can query (with strict consent) to provide perfectly tailored products and avoid health risks:
- **Body Measurements:** Secure storage of 3D body scans, traditional tailoring measurements, and brand-specific sizing preferences to ensure accurate fits and drastically reduce e-commerce return rates.
- **Orthotics & Podiatry:** Prescribed orthotic requirements, arch support needs, specific foot dimensions, and gait analysis records for customized footwear.
- **Material & Fabric Allergies:** Verified medical or personal notes detailing skin sensitivities or allergies (e.g., to latex, specific synthetic dyes, nickel, or wool). The retailer's system can read this data to automatically warn the customer before they purchase an incompatible product.

### 2. Transactional & Lifecycle Records
Moving away from easily lost paper receipts and siloed email confirmations, the Solid Databox serves as the definitive repository for the lifecycle of purchased goods:
- **Receipt & Provenance Information:** Cryptographically verifiable digital receipts proving ownership, purchase date, and the authenticity/provenance of goods (which is critical for high-end fashion, sustainability tracking, and the secondary resale market).
- **Warranty & Repair Logs:** Active, structured tracking of warranty periods, repair histories, and return/exchange eligibility directly tied to the product's digital receipt.

### 3. Customer Engagement & Loyalty
- **Loyalty Relationship Supports:** A decentralized approach to loyalty programs. Instead of carrying physical cards or managing dozens of fragmented store accounts, the user's Solid Pod stores their loyalty status, accumulated points, and VIP membership tiers as verifiable credentials.
- **Messaging & Promotional Supports:** A secure, consent-driven messaging channel. Retailers can send tailored promotional information, exclusive product drops, or important product recall notices directly to the customer's databox inbox. This entirely bypasses traditional email spam filters and allows the consumer to instantly revoke the brand's messaging access at any time.

## The Solid Databox Workflow

1. **Consent & Connection:** The customer links their Solid Pod to the retailer's Point of Sale (POS) or e-commerce platform.
2. **Contextual Read:** The retailer requests scoped read-access to the customer's body measurements, orthotic requirements, and fabric allergies to dynamically filter the product catalog and recommend perfect fits.
3. **Transactional Write:** Upon purchase, the retailer deposits the digital receipt, warranty information, and updated loyalty points directly into the customer's Solid Databox.
4. **Ongoing Lifecycle:** The retailer utilizes the databox's messaging supports for post-purchase care and promotions, while the customer retains absolute control over their data footprint and the brand relationship.
