# Community: Financial Counselling & Welfare Services

Financial counselling and pro-bono financial services assist individuals facing bankruptcy, severe debt, or economic abuse. These services require deep, invasive access to a client's financial reality to negotiate with creditors or access hardship funds. Traditionally, this involves insecurely handing over boxes of paper receipts or raw login credentials to bank accounts. A Solid Databox standardizes and secures this process.

## Core Financial Welfare Payloads

### 1. Hardship & Solvency Records
- **Granular Financial Snapshots:** Clients can generate specific Verifiable Credentials (VCs) from their banking institution that cryptographically prove insolvency, default notices, or extreme financial hardship, without exposing every single line-item of their daily spending to the NGO.
- **Economic Abuse Documentation:** In complex cases of domestic violence, a victim can securely store VCs proving coerced debt or financial control (e.g., frozen assets, manipulated credit files). This immutable record is critical for advocates when negotiating with banks to wipe fraudulent or coerced debts.

### 2. Creditor Negotiation & Hardship Grants
- **Verified Hardship Claims:** Financial counsellors can issue a "Verified Hardship Assessment" VC to the client’s Databox. The client can seamlessly share this credential with utility providers, telcos, and retail banks to automatically trigger mandatory hardship provisions or halt aggressive debt collection instantly.
- **Grant Applications:** When applying for emergency relief grants (e.g., to replace a broken fridge or pay an imminent eviction notice), the Databox provides instant, cryptographic proof of the impending crisis (like an eviction VC), massively speeding up the disbursement of NGO funds.

### 3. Agent Trust & Delegation
- **Delegated Negotiation (Digital Power of Attorney):** The client can issue a cryptographic mandate—acting as a digital letter of authority—granting the financial counsellor the explicit, time-bound right to negotiate with creditors on their behalf. The counselor presents this token to the bank, proving they have lawful authorization to act for the client without needing physical signatures or wet-ink documents.
