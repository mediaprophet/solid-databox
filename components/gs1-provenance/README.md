# Components: GS1 & Product Provenance

Associating real-world physical goods with digital ledgers is critical for the retail, health, and ethical consumption verticals. This component leverages global supply chain standards—notably GS1 barcodes and Digital Links—to bring physical product data into the Databox ecosystem.

## Use Cases

### 1. Health Monitoring & Allergies
- By scanning a GS1 barcode (or a 2D QR code encoded with GS1 Digital Link) via their Databox-linked app, a consumer instantly cross-references the product's exact, standardized ingredient list against the verified allergy VCs stored in their Pod. 
- The app can instantly alert them if a product contains traces of peanuts, gluten, or other flagged allergens before purchase.

### 2. Product Recalls & Food Safety
- When an item is purchased, the GS1 product identifier (GTIN) and batch number are embedded into the digital receipt (VC) stored in the Databox.
- If a manufacturer issues a health recall (e.g., salmonella-contaminated spinach or a faulty electrical appliance), the manufacturer or retailer can push a notification directly to the specific Databoxes that hold a receipt for the affected batch. This bypasses the need for highly ineffective public notice campaigns and social media blasts.

### 3. Ethical Provenance & Sustainability
- Scanning products reveals verifiable claims about the supply chain (e.g., carbon footprint, Fair Trade certification, conflict-free materials). Consumers can aggregate this data in their Pod to track the environmental impact of their ongoing purchasing habits, ensuring corporate accountability.
